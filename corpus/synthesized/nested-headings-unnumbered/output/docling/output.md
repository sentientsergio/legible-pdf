## Apple

The apple is a pomaceous fruit cultivated worldwide for its crisp texture and balanced sweetness. Cultivars vary in color, sugar content, and storage characteristics, with some varieties bred specifically for cooking and others for fresh eating.

## Banana

The banana is an elongated tropical fruit produced by several species of large herbaceous flowering plants in the genus Musa. Bananas are often eaten raw and are rich in potassium and dietary fiber.

## Cherry

The cherry is a small drupe with a single hard seed. Cherries are produced by trees of the genus Prunus and are valued both for fresh consumption and for use in preserves.

## Date

The date is the fruit of the date palm tree. Dates are sweet and dense, with a chewy texture, and have been a staple food in arid regions for thousands of years.

## Elderberry

The elderberry is a small dark berry produced by elder shrubs of the genus Sambucus. Elderberries are most commonly used in syrups, jams, and wines rather than eaten fresh.

## Fig

The fig is a soft, sweet fruit with edible skin and many small seeds. Figs are produced by ficus trees and are eaten fresh, dried, or preserved.

## Grape

The grape is a non-climacteric berry that grows in clusters on woody perennial vines of the genus Vitis. Grapes are cultivated for fresh consumption, drying into raisins, and most extensively for wine production.

## Honeydew

The honeydew is a melon cultivar with smooth pale-green flesh and sweet flavor. Honeydews are typically eaten fresh and are valued for their high water content.

## Iceberg

The iceberg, while commonly known as a lettuce variety rather than a fruit, is included here as a recognizable produce name. It is crisp and watery, with mild flavor.
